How it works

A simple diagram here showing CREATE->(MAKE->ANSWER->SCORE) would be good.

CREATE - The host creates the game by picking a theme and inviting a group of players. Here are some example themes:

  • "Class of '87" -- played with your high school or college friends.
  • "The Smith Family" -- about your own family leading up to a reunion.
  • "The Gators Summer 2013 Season" -- reminisce over the last season with your team mates.

MAKE - One player ('the author') makes a multiple choice question that some players should know and may challenge others. Pictures can be included.

ANSWER - The other players answer the question in a timed session. Players are notified (by email for example) when it is time to take their turn, and can play at their leisure.

SCORE - After all players have answered the score is calculated. The scoring is designed to reward players for answering correctly, and punish authors if they make a question that no one could answer.

REPEAT - The game goes through the cycle of make->answer->score until each player has a turn at being the author one time (or more for longer games).

Example questions:

Taken a game played between the guests coming to Thanksgiving dinner at Ken and Jamies's house:

I have to admit sometimes when a baby picture comes up in a random slide show of our family photos I struggle to figure out who it is. This is one of those pictures. Can you tell who it is? - by You

  •   1: Saire Caitlin Micaela
  • 2: Ronan Jamie Samuel Scott
  •   3: Declan
  •   ?: I give up Susan

Time Limit: 60 seconds

Hint: 'Who's who (Jamie you better get this!)'

Explanation: 'That's Ronan in October of 2011 at 6 months. I hope someone else had trouble with this too or I am going to feel like a bad dad.'

Comments:

"Haha I changed my mind at the last minute and got it wrong, it could be any one of them!" - Caitlin

"Are you kidding? I knew it was supposed to be one of your kids, but my first thought was that it was a picture of Jamie." - Susan

"I almost put Saire but I'll never confess!" - Jamie

Taken from a game of friends remininicing about their days on the Bronx Science Speech team:

Which accent did I do a poor impression of for a drama reading? - by Micaela

  •   1: A young Irish hussy
  • 2: A dippy Southern belle Slavica Katharine Sharda
  •   3: An gruff Australian grandmother Sonya
  •   4: A wise Mexican soothsayer
  •   ?: I give up

Time Limit: 60 seconds

Hint: 'Meryl Streep I am not!'

Explanation: 'I played Babe from "Crimes of the Heart" which takes place in Mississippi. In the scene Babe tries to kill herself by climbing in the oven after shooting her husband. Sissy Spacek played her in the film version. Pretty sure my southern relatives would have been unimpressed by my accent!'

Comments

"Oh come now! I seem to remember your Southern accent as one of your best! Ha ha, I love that you remember the details of your scene so well!" - Slavica

"You have a great accent!" - Katharine

"You did dippy Southern belles proud!" - Sharda

"Also I love that you incorporated the word "hussy" into the choices. " - Sharda

"those adjectives were highly distracting - almost went with Mexican ;)" - Sonya
